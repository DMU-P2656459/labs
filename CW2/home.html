Guitarist of the Week

Our pick for Guitarist of the day today is Jimi Hendrix. Guitar legend Jimi Hendrix was born in Seattle on November 27th, 1942 and died on September 18, 1970. Hendrix grew up playing guitar, imitating blues greats like Muddy Waters. In the early 1960s, Hendrix worked as a pickup guitarist, backing musicians including B.B. King, Ike and Tina Turner, and Little Richard. In 1964, he moved to New York, where bassist Bryan Chandler of the British group the Animals heard him. They created the Jimi Hendrix Experience with bassist Noel Redding and drummer Mitch Mitchell. The band’s first single, “Hey Joe,” hit No. 6 on the British pop charts, and the band became an instant sensation. Hendrix was quickly established as a rock superstar. The band released many classic songs such as “Purple Haze,” “Foxy Lady,” and “The Wind Cries Mary.”. Jimi Hendrix has influenced many guitarists since his death. Clearly displaying the legend, he truly is.

Band of the Week

Our band of the week this week is Sonic Youth! Sonic Youth, are an American avant-garde noise band and highly influential forerunner of the alternative rock groups of the 1980s and ’90s. The principal members were Kim Gordon, Lee Ranaldo, Thurston Moore, and Steve Shelley. Check out the video above to see Sonic Youth in action, performing in the Basement in 2020!
